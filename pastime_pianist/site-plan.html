Content

Home page

Hi there, thanks for stopping by! I'm Ryan, a student and pianist located in the Rexburg area. If you're looking for a piano accompanist for a choir, musical number, or other performance, then I might be able to help you out! I'm an accomplished pianist with experience as an accompanist and piano teacher. I love making music with people and making new connections with people. I'm also a composer and arranger, and it's one of my favorite things (hence, this website). If you want a song arranged for you to perform, or if you have a project in need of original music, reach out to me and let's talk! A word of warning; I am a college student with a busy course load, so I will need to make sure me and my schedule can handle helping out before I commit to anything. And, while I am accomplished at the piano, I am still in the process of developing my own skills, so I need to review any music I would need to play before I can commit. Practice takes time, and while I would love to help out everywhere, sometimes the music might be better suited for another musician.

FAQ's

Here are some answers to questions that I've been asked about this in the past: How long have you been playing piano? 12 years Where did you learn to compose and arrange from? There's a lot behind that question. Having a good background in piano has definitely played a big part in it. A lot of music theory goes into it, so learning that was important. A big part of it was a friend of mine showing me how to work with the chords of a song and kind of make it up as you go. That was a big turning point for me and helped me get started with really composing and arranging. But really the best way to learn is to just start doing it. For a long time I was hoping to find a class or website or video that would teach me exactly what I wanted to know. But in the end, it was really just by doing it that i figured it out. I still have a lot to learn though, so if you have a background in music or composition, I'd appreciate any constructive criticism you have to offer. What program do you use to arrange? To make nice looking sheet music, I use MuseScore 4, which is totally free and you should definitely check it out if you're looking for a good notation program. But I prefer to start using pencil and paper to work on music. I work it all out on paper, then use MuseScore as a sort of spell check to help make sure all of my rhythms and notations are correct.

Compositions and Arrangements

In my spare time, I like to work on composing and arranging music. I bounce around a lot in regards to genre; I've done musical theater, music from game and movie soundtracks, simplified pop songs for my piano students, and some joke music (which is one of my personal favorites.). I have a long list of ideas for future composing/arranging projects that I plan to work on in the future, but I'm always open to new ideas, so if there is a song you would like me to consider, send me a message. Learning to compose and arrange has been a long and not always enjoyable process. It's taken a lot of years of piano lessons and trial and error for me to get to where I am today (and honestly, I still have a long way to go). Playing music is tricky, but putting together that tricky-to-play music is another thing altogether. So if you want to learn to compose for yourself and you don't know where to start, send me your questions and concerns and I will try to help as much as I can. Or if you are already working on a piece and just want a second opinion, I can give it a look and give some constructive criticism and advice. Music takes a lot of trial and error (and I've experienced it), but it's always worth it in the end. So keep working on those projects and let me know how I can help.
