How long have you been playing piano?

12 years and counting

Where did you learn to write/arrange music?

There’s a lot behind that question. I’ve learned through experience that there isn’t really one place to go to learn. Having a good background in piano has definitely played a big part in it. A lot of music theory goes into it, so learning that was important. A large part of it was a friend of mine showing me how to work with the chords of a song and kind of make it up as you go. That was a major turning point for me and helped me get started with really composing and arranging. But really the best way to learn is to just start doing it. For a long time I was hoping to find a class or website or video that would teach me exactly what I wanted to know. But ultimately, it was by just sitting down and trying to do it that helped me the most. I still have a lot to learn though, so if you have a background in music or composition, I’d appreciate any constructive criticism you have to offer.

What program do you use to arrange?

To make nice looking sheet music, I use MuseScore 4, which is totally free and you should definitely check it out if you’re looking for a good notation program. But I prefer to start using pencil and paper to work on music. I work it all out on paper, then use MuseScore as a sort of spell check to help make sure all of my rhythms and notations are correct.
